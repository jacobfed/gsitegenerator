Visualization and Quantification of Small Airways Disease.


Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD) is characterized by incompletely reversible airflow limitations that hinders normal breathing. Small airways disease, a treatable component of COPD, accounts for most of the pulmonary dysfunction in patients with mild to moderate disease. Using standard of care paired CT scans, our technique, called Parametric Response Mapping, is capable of visualizing and quantifying the SAD in the presence of emphysema.

Predicting Spirometric Decline in COPD Patients


COPD patients demonstrate varying rates of disease progression. Some may show a stable condition where treatments manage the conditions, we as others may progress rapidly losing nearly all pulmonary function over a short period of time. Early knowledge of the rate of progression will aid physicians in treating their patients. We have demonstrated that SAD, as measured by PRM, is predictive of spirometric decline in patients with mild to moderate COPD.

Quantifying the Spatial heterogeneity of SAD


Emphysema, irreversible alveolar destruction, is a leading cause of mortality in this population as well as being identified as a predictor of poor survival. Associated with severe COPD, emphysema advances slowly with limited treatment options once diagnosed. Evidence of small airways disease as an emphysema precursor. This highlights the potential importance of the independent and non-invasive evaluation of fSAD through PRM. We have developed a technique that is an extension of the PRM approach that extracts local topological features from PRM-derived disease classifications maps.
